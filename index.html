What's Happening?

Modular arithmetic is like arithmetic on a clock. When we add or multiply in ℤ/nℤ, we wrap around after reaching n.

Think of it: If it's 10 o'clock and you add 5 hours, you get 3 o'clock (not 15 o'clock).

Real-world uses: Cryptography, computer science, music theory, and calendar calculations all use modular arithmetic.
